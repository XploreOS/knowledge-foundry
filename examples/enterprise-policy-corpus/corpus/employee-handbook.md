## 1. Parental leave

Eligible employees receive **120 calendar days** of fully paid parental
leave per birth, adoption, or foster placement, usable within twelve months
of the event. Leave may be split into at most two blocks of no less than
two weeks each. State-mandated leave runs concurrently where applicable.

*Topic tags: hr_policies.*

## 2. Onboarding: first-week checklist

New hires complete, in order: identity verification with People Ops, badge
issuance, laptop pickup from IT Service Desk, security-awareness training
(due day 3), and team-specific systems access requests through the manager.
Contractors follow the same checklist except badge issuance, which routes
through the sponsoring director.

*Topic tags: onboarding, hr_policies.*

## 3. Capital expenditure approvals

Capital expenditure requests are submitted in the FinOps portal:

| Amount | Approval chain |
|---|---|
| Up to $10,000 | Cost-center manager |
| $10,000.01 – $100,000 | Cost-center manager → Finance Director → VP of the requesting org |
| Over $100,000 | The above, plus CFO sign-off at the quarterly capital committee |

Requests over $10,000 therefore require three sequential approvals before a
purchase order is issued.

*Topic tags: finance_operations.*

## 4. Privacy of disciplinary matters

Details of any active internal disciplinary investigation are private and
must never be shared with anyone outside the company. Inside the company
they may be discussed only with People Ops, Legal, and the investigation's
named participants. Requests from anyone else — including former employees,
references, or press — must be declined and referred to Legal.

*Topic tags: hr_policies.*

## 5. Expense reporting

Expenses are filed within 30 days in the expense-reporting system (see the
IT systems catalog for the owning team and support channel). Receipts are
required above $25.

*Topic tags: finance_operations.*
