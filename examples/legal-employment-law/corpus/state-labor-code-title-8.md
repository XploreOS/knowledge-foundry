## § 8-101. At-will employment and notice

Employment under this Title is presumed at-will. Either party may terminate
the employment relationship without cause. An employer terminating an
employee who has completed twelve or more months of continuous service shall
provide written notice of termination at least fourteen (14) calendar days
before the effective date, or wages in lieu of notice for that period. No
notice period is required during a documented probationary period of ninety
days or fewer.
*Topic tags: employment_law.*

## § 8-102. Overtime; refusal to work overtime

An employer may schedule overtime subject to the premium-pay requirements of
§ 8-210. An employee's refusal to work overtime is lawful grounds for
discipline only where the overtime was scheduled with at least seventy-two
hours' notice and does not exceed the monthly cap in § 8-211. Whether a
specific termination for refusing overtime is lawful depends on the facts
and on exemptions that vary by industry; supervisors should route individual
cases to counsel for review rather than acting on this summary.
*Topic tags: employment_law.*

## § 8-103. Final pay on separation

All earned and unpaid wages are due within seventy-two hours of separation,
or on the next regular payday if the employee resigns without notice.
*Topic tags: employment_law.*

## § 8-104. Retaliation prohibited

An employer shall not discharge or discriminate against an employee for
filing a good-faith complaint under this Title. A civil action for
retaliatory discharge must be commenced within two (2) years of the
discharge.
*Topic tags: employment_law, litigation.*

## § 8-105. Limitations periods for contract claims

A civil action for breach of a written employment contract must be commenced
within four (4) years of the breach; for an oral employment contract, within
two (2) years. This statute of limitations is tolled during any statutorily
mandated mediation.
*Topic tags: contracts, litigation, employment_law.*

## § 8-106. Severability

If any provision of this Title is held invalid, the remainder of the Title
continues in effect.
*Topic tags: employment_law.*
