## Part 1 — Regulatory filings before a consumer product launch

Before a new consumer product may be offered for sale in this jurisdiction,
a seller must complete regulatory filings with the Office of Consumer
Affairs, listed below.
*Topic tags: regulatory_compliance.*

1. **Form CP-1, Product Registration** — filed at least thirty days before
   the first offer for sale, describing the product, its intended use, and
   its safety testing summary.
2. **Form CP-2, Labeling Certification** — certifying that packaging meets
   the plain-language labeling rule (Reg. § 12.4).
3. **Fee schedule filing** — only for products sold on a subscription basis.

Products regulated by a federal agency (food, drugs, vehicles) are exempt
from CP-1 but not from CP-2. Late registration is subject to a per-day civil
penalty and may support a deceptive-practices claim.
*Topic tags: regulatory_compliance.*

## Part 2 — Elements of a trade secret misappropriation claim

Under the Exampleton Uniform Trade Secrets Act, a plaintiff must prove three
elements to establish a trade secret misappropriation claim: existence of a
trade secret, misappropriation, and damages or threatened harm.
*Topic tags: intellectual_property, litigation.*

1. **Existence of a trade secret** — information deriving independent
   economic value from not being generally known, subject to reasonable
   secrecy measures;
2. **Misappropriation** — acquisition by improper means, or disclosure/use
   by a person who knew or should have known the information was acquired
   improperly or under a duty of confidence;
3. **Damages or threatened harm** — actual loss, unjust enrichment, or the
   basis for injunctive relief.

Reasonable secrecy measures are assessed as of the time of the alleged
misappropriation, not the time of filing.
*Topic tags: intellectual_property.*

## Part 3 — Status of the leading misappropriation case

The leading decision on this legal issue, *Northfield Instruments v. Kale*
(Ex. Sup. Ct. 2019), remains good law on elements one and two, but its
damages holding was narrowed by a later decision, *Verdant Labs v. Ossler*
(Ex. Ct. App. 2024), which limits unjust-enrichment recovery to the
misappropriation window. Check whether a case has been overruled or narrowed
before citing *Northfield* on remedies.
*Topic tags: litigation, intellectual_property.*
