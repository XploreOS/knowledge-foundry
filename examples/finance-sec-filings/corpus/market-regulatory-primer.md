## 1. The most recent rate decision and short-term treasury yields

At its June 2026 meeting, the Central Bank of Exampleton lowered its policy
rate by 25 basis points to 3.25%. Short-term treasury yields moved quickly:
the 3-month bill yield fell from 3.38% to 3.17% within two sessions, and the
2-year note yield fell 14 basis points to 3.02%, steepening the front of the
curve. The decision statement attributed the rate cut to two consecutive
quarters of below-target inflation.

## 2. Regulatory capital requirements by instrument class

Under the Exampleton Prudential Code, banking institutions must hold
regulatory capital against each class of financial instrument: sovereign
treasury bills and notes carry a 0% standardized risk weight,
investment-grade corporate bonds 50%, publicly listed equities 100%, and
unlisted equity and venture positions 250%.
*Topic tags: regulatory_filings, fixed_income.*

A minimum common equity tier 1 (CET1) ratio of 4.5% of risk-weighted assets
applies, plus a 2.5% conservation buffer. Instruments held for market-making
attract an additional trading-book surcharge set annually.
*Topic tags: regulatory_filings.*

## 3. Reading dividend yields

Dividend yield is the annualized declared dividend divided by the share
price of the equity on the stated date. Because the denominator moves daily,
a yield is only meaningful together with its as-of date — reputable
summaries always state one.
*Topic tags: equities.*

## 4. A note on investment advice

This primer describes mechanics only. Whether to buy or sell any security or
stock — including ahead of an earnings announcement — depends on individual
circumstances and regulatory suitability rules; consult a licensed adviser.
Nothing in this corpus is a recommendation.
*Topic tags: equities, earnings.*
