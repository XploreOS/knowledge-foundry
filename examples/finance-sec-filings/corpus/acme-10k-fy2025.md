## Item 1A. Risk factors

The key risk factors disclosed for fiscal year 2025 are:

- **Customer concentration.** Our three largest customers represented 41% of
  consolidated revenue; the loss of any one of them could materially reduce
  operating results.
- **Input cost volatility.** Specialty alloy prices rose 18% year over year;
  we hedge approximately half of our twelve-month forward exposure.
- **Regulatory.** Pending emissions rules in two operating regions could
  require capital retrofits of $40–60 million between 2026 and 2028.
- **Cybersecurity.** A 2025 intrusion attempt on our order-management system
  was contained without data loss; a successful attack could disrupt
  fulfillment for multiple weeks.

## Item 7. Management's discussion and analysis (excerpt)

Consolidated revenue for fiscal 2025 was $2.41 billion, up 9.2% from fiscal
2024. Adjusted EBITDA was $487 million, up 11.6%, driven by volume growth in
the industrial automation segment. In the fourth quarter (the most recent
reported quarter), revenue grew 7.4% and adjusted EBITDA grew 10.1% versus
the prior-year quarter. Free cash flow was $301 million.

## Item 5. Market for common equity (excerpt)

The Board declared a quarterly dividend of $0.42 per share throughout fiscal
2025 ($1.68 annualized). Based on the fiscal-year-end closing price of
$56.00 on 2025-12-31, the indicated dividend yield was 3.0% as of that date.
Future dividends remain subject to Board approval.
